Performance Venues

Bergen Community College Performing Arts Series

Anna Maria Ciccone Theatre
400 Paramus Rd.
Paramus, NJ 07652
www.bergen.edu
201.447.7100

The Ciccone Theatre offers first class entertainment by world class performing artists at the main theatre at Bergen Community College. The proscenium theatre seats 300 and rivals any off-Broadway theatre in New York.

Bergen County Players Inc.

298 Kinderkamack Rd.
Oradell, NJ 07649
www.bcplayers.org
201.261.4200

From its roots as a small community theater, the Bergen County Players has grown into a little theater with a big reputation for quality productions. As the Players marks its 80th anniversary this 2012-13 season, the troupe celebrates its rich history, which includes musicals, comedy, drama and suspense. Robert Sean Leonard, Tony Award winner and star of Fox television’s hit drama “House.” Broadway actress Beth Fowler, of “Sweeney Todd” and “The Boy from Oz,” who also originated the role of Mrs. Potts in “Beauty and the Beast” are some of the now famous faces that earlier in their careers graced the stage.

Bergen Performing Arts Center (bergenPAC)

30 N. Van Brunt St.
Englewood, NJ 07631
www.bergenpac.org
201.816.8160

The Bergen Performing Arts Center (bergenPAC) is northern New Jersey’s most vibrant not-for-profit performing arts center – a home to hundreds of world class performances at affordable prices reaching audiences of over 250,000 each year. bergenPAC is also a home to Grammy winning recordings and broadcasts distributed internationally. However, the soul of bergenPAC can best be seen in our efforts to provide a home for innovative arts and educational programs that reach out to thousands of families.

Center for Modern Dance Education

84 Euclid Ave.
Hackensack, NJ 07601
www.cmde.org
201.342.2989

The Center for Modern Dance Education (CMDE) is a non-profit community arts organization. The mission of the Center for Modern Dance Education (CMDE) is to promote modern dance in partnership with the community and make it available to everyone.

The Center seeks, through quality dance programs, to nurture and instill confidence and self-acceptance in each student, regardless of physical or financial ability, including individuals with disabilities, youth at risk, older adults, and economically disadvantaged and underserved populations; and to preserve the legacy of modern dance and nurture its future.

Garage Theatre at Becton Theatre

960 River Rd. Fairleigh Dickinson University
Teaneck, NJ 07666
www.garagetheatre.org
201-569-7710

The Garage Theatre Group is in residence at the Becton Theatre, on the Hackensack River side of FDU's Becton Hall. GTG produces professional productions, as well as Youth Conservatory musicals, and various special events.

Fair Lawn Community Center

10-10 20th Street
Fair Lawn, New Jersey 07410
www.fairlawncommunitycenter.org
201-794-5372

The public will enjoy world class plays, musicals, concerts and more at the Fair Lawn Community Center. They have partnered with the Hurdy Gurdy Folk Music Club to bring in some of the best musicians in folk music. Also home to the Old Library Theatre and co-sponsors to St. Anne Stages, New Vision Players and Skyline Theatre Company which now make their home at the Center. Programs also presented by the Fair Lawn Russian Club.

Puffin Cultural Forum

20 Puffin Way
Teaneck, NJ
www.puffinculturalforum.org
201.836.3499

The Puffin Cultural Forum, a project of the Puffin Foundation Ltd., is the gallery and performance space located at the site of the Foundation's administrative offices in Teaneck, New Jersey. The work takes place at the intersection of the arts and the struggle for human rights as we seek to realize in our local community the Foundation's governing motto: "...continuing the dialogue between art and the lives of ordinary people".

The Saddle River Valley Cultural Center (SRVCC) was formed in 1982 to present cultural events and to increase awareness and appreciation of the fine arts. At the same time, the organization is preserving the old Saddle River Methodist Church, built in 1842, as an historical site

West Side Presbyterian Church

6 South Monroe St
Ridgewood, NJ 07450
www.westside.org
201-652-1966 Ext. 30

Throughout the long history of the series, West Side Presbyterian Church has offered a varied palette of solo, instrumental and ensemble concerts in the classical, African-American and jazz traditions. Since its installation in October of 2007, the new, three-manual Nichols & Simpson Sanctuary Pipe Organ has been featured in each season. The acoustically favorable sanctuary also houses a Steinway Grand Piano and a Peter Fisk two-manual harpsichord, instruments used frequently in the series.
